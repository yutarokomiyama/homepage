  • High-Frequency Power Electronics

    •    Most of the applications around us run on "electricity" as its energy source. Electricity is widely used in modern society because it can easily change amount and form, and is suitable for transportation. The field of practical engineering that deals with the conversion of electrical energy is called power electronics.
       To date, power electronics have grown and evolved following the development of "power semiconductors." Power semiconductors are one of the essential components used in power converters. They determine the overall performance of the power converter. Therefore, new power electronics technologies have been developed to correspond to the emergence of new power semiconductors. In recent years, high-performance power semiconductors based on Silicon Carbide (SiC) and Gallium Nitride (GaN) have attracted much attention. These power semiconductors can operate at higher power and frequency than conventional devices.
       My research is about high-frequency power electronics using power semiconductors such as SiC and GaN. By increasing the frequency of the power converters, passive components such as capacitors and inductors can be made smaller and lighter. Therefore, unprecedented compact and lightweight power converters are expected to be realized in this research.
